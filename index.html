About Me

I am a recent graduate of Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) with a Bachelor's degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering, where I achieved a CGPA of 3.77/4.00. My passion lies at the intersection of electronics and biotechnology, with a strong research focus on nanoelectronic devices for biomedical applications. My undergraduate thesis, which involved designing novel FET-based biosensors, ignited my desire to pursue a PhD and contribute to the development of next-generation diagnostic tools. I am actively seeking a challenging PhD position where I can apply my skills in semiconductor device physics and fabrication to solve real-world problems in healthcare.

Research Projects

  • Thesis: Designing the Cavity Architecture in Double Gate Junctionless Field Effect Transistor for Enhanced Biomolecule Detection: My undergraduate thesis, supervised by Dr. Ehsanur Rahman.
  • Polar Gate Engineering and Cavity Optimization in JL-TFET Biosensors for Ultra-Sensitive Label-Free Detection: Investigating optimal placement of the polar gate by analyzing various biosensing parameters to identify the best configuration.
  • Comparative Design of TMDC and Si-based Junctionless TFETs for Biosensing Applications: Evaluating TMDC materials versus silicon in junctionless TFETs for biomolecule detection using identical device parameters.
  • Automated Experimental Setup to Measure Optical Power-Current Characteristics of LEDs (Under Review): Developed a low-cost Arduino-driven setup to measure LED L-I characteristics.
  • 8:1 Analog Multiplexer Design in Cadence Virtuoso: Designed an 8:1 analog multiplexer using hierarchical 2:1 blocks with selector logic and transmission gates.
  • Hybrid 4-Way Traffic Control System Using 74-Series ICs: Designed a 4-way traffic control system with jam management and priority handling.
  • Automated Greenhouse Control System Using Feedback: Developed an Arduino UNO-based greenhouse prototype integrating sensors for climate and soil regulation.
